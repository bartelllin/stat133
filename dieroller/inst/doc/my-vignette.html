Die Rolling

The package "Dieroller" is an implementation for simulating rolling a die a given number of times, and to visualize the relative frequencies in a series of such die rolls.

Creating a die

The first step is to create a "die" object with the function die():

fair <- die()
fair
#> object "die"
#> 
#>   sides      prob
#> 1     1 0.1666667
#> 2     2 0.1666667
#> 3     3 0.1666667
#> 4     4 0.1666667
#> 5     5 0.1666667
#> 6     6 0.1666667

By default, the function creates a die with sides 1-6, each with probablility 1/6.

Rolling

You can roll the die with the roll() function:

fair_roll <- roll(fair, 50)
fair_roll
#> object "roll"
#> 
#> $rolls
#>  [1] 1 6 6 6 2 5 3 6 6 6 4 3 2 4 5 3 4 1 6 3 4 2 6 4 6 2 1 5 1 6 6 5 2 5 2
#> [36] 4 3 2 4 1 1 4 2 6 5 5 1 6 2 3

Plotting

You can plot the rolls as well:

plot(fair_roll)

Summary

You can even view a summary of the rolls:

summary(fair_roll)
#> summary "roll
#> 
#> $freqs
#>   side count prop
#> 1    1     7 0.14
#> 2    2     9 0.18
#> 3    3     6 0.12
#> 4    4     8 0.16
#> 5    5     7 0.14
#> 6    6    13 0.26
